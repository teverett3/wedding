Getting to Montauk

About 2.5 Hours from NYC

We recommend arriving by midday Friday, October 23rd to enjoy the weekend activities.

By Car:
2.5–3 hours from NYC via I-495 E and NY-27 E. Rental cars recommended for exploring the area. Complimentary parking at Gurney's.
By Train:
LIRR from Penn Station to Montauk (~3 hours). Uber/taxi available from station to resort.
By Bus:
Hampton Jitney offers service from Manhattan to Montauk.

Explore the Hamptons

Make a long weekend of it. From Montauk's rugged coastline to the charm of Sag Harbor and the elegance of East Hampton, there's so much to see and do.

Sightseeing

Montauk

Montauk Point Lighthouse

The oldest lighthouse in New York, perched at the easternmost tip of Long Island. Panoramic ocean views and a small museum inside.

Springs

Pollock-Krasner House

The studio and home of Jackson Pollock and Lee Krasner. A must-see for art lovers — the paint-splattered studio floor is iconic.

Water Mill

Parrish Art Museum

World-class museum showcasing American art with a stunning Herzog & de Meuron–designed building. Rotating exhibitions and beautiful grounds.

Beaches

Montauk

Ditch Plains Beach

Montauk's most beloved beach. Great for surfing, walking, and watching the waves. Laid-back locals-only energy.

East Hampton

Georgica Beach

Wide, pristine stretch of sand in one of the Hamptons' most beautiful settings. Quieter and tucked away — perfect for a peaceful morning.

Montauk

Gerard Drive Beach

A hidden gem at the end of Gerard Drive on Fort Pond Bay. Calm water, gorgeous sunsets, and a local secret worth finding.

Vineyards

Sagaponack

Wölffer Estate Vineyard

Iconic Hamptons winery with beautiful grounds, a tasting room, and their famous rosé. The perfect afternoon stop.

Bridgehampton

Channing Daughters Winery

Boutique winery known for creative, small-batch wines and a charming sculpture garden. A more intimate tasting experience.

Dining

Bridgehampton

Almond

French-American bistro that's a Hamptons staple. Warm atmosphere, excellent food, and the kind of place you want to linger.

Bridgehampton

Topping Rose House

Farm-to-table fine dining at a beautifully restored 19th-century house. Chef Jean-Georges Vongerichten's Hamptons outpost.

Amagansett

Il Buco al Mare

Italian coastal cuisine from the team behind Manhattan's Il Buco. Fresh pasta, seafood, and a chic beachy setting.

Montauk

Rosie's

Lively Montauk spot with a great outdoor scene, creative cocktails, and a fun, social atmosphere. Perfect for a casual night out.

Activities

Golf

Montauk Downs State Park

Renowned public course designed by Robert Trent Jones. Considered one of the best public courses in the state — book a tee time early.

Spa

Shou Sugi Ban House

A serene wellness retreat in the Hamptons offering Japanese-inspired spa treatments, sound baths, and forest bathing experiences.

Horseback Riding

Deep Hollow Ranch

The oldest cattle ranch in the United States, right in Montauk. Trail rides through rolling pastures with ocean views.

Hikes & Nature

Montauk

Camp Hero State Park

Former military base with hiking trails, dramatic coastal bluffs, and the iconic radar tower. Easy trails with incredible ocean views.

Montauk

Shadmoor State Park

Cliffside trails overlooking the Atlantic with wildflowers and WWII-era bunkers. A short, beautiful walk minutes from town.

Amagansett

Walking Dunes Trail

A unique preserve with shifting sand dunes, a freshwater bog, and panoramic views of Napeague Harbor. Unlike anywhere else on Long Island.

Shopping

East Hampton

Main Street & Newtown Lane

The Hamptons' luxury shopping destination. Tiffany, Ralph Lauren, Clic Gallery, and more — all set along tree-lined streets.

Sag Harbor

Sag Harbor Village

Charming boutiques, independent bookshops, and art galleries in a picturesque whaling village. More laid-back and artsy than East Hampton.

Montauk

Downtown Montauk

Surf shops, local boutiques, and casual cafés. Pick up Montauk gear, handmade jewelry, or just grab a coffee and stroll.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I RSVP?

Please RSVP by August 1, 2026. You can respond to your invitation or contact us directly.

Is there parking at the venue?

Yes, Gurney's offers complimentary parking for all wedding guests.

What's the weather like in October?

Montauk in late October is beautiful! Average temperatures are 55-65°F. We recommend bringing a light jacket for the evening ocean breeze.

Are children welcome?

While we love your little ones, we've planned an adults-only celebration to allow all guests to relax and enjoy the evening.

Will there be transportation?

For guests staying at Gurney's, no transportation is needed. For those staying elsewhere, we recommend Uber or taxi services.

What if I have dietary restrictions?

Please let us know about any dietary restrictions when you RSVP, and we'll work with the venue to accommodate your needs.

We're here to help! Feel free to reach out to us directly.
